Overview

Rule 30 is a one-dimentional Cellular Automaton devised by Stephen Wolfram in 1983. It consists of an infinite one-dimentional array of binary elements.

To begin with each cell has a state 0 or 1, then at each time step each cell changes state depending on it's own and its two neighbour's states.

Wolfram

Stephen Wolfram was a British-American computer scientist and physicist who was born in london but spent most of his professional career in the Unites States.

When he created the Rule 30 Automaton, Wolfram was working as a researcher at The Institude for Advanced Study in Princeton, where he specialised in Cellular Automata.
